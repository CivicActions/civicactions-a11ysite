# Join Us

How to get involved with CivicActions Accessibility, whether you're already part of the team or interested in joining our mission.

## Already Working for CivicActions?

### Become an Accessibility Champion
Join our internal [Champions Program](/champion-accessibility/champions-program) to:
- **Build accessibility expertise** in your practice area
- **Advocate for inclusive design** on your projects  
- **Connect with the accessibility community** beyond CivicActions
- **Advance your career** in accessibility and inclusive technology

**Next steps**: Contact the accessibility practice leads or express interest through internal channels.

### Contribute to This Site
This site is **open source** and welcomes contributions from CivicActions team members:
- **Improve existing content** based on your project experience
- **Share case studies** from successful accessibility implementations
- **Add new resources** you've found valuable
- **Update outdated information** or broken links

**Repository**: [github.com/mgifford/civicactions-a11ysite](https://github.com/mgifford/civicactions-a11ysite)

### Get Involved in Projects
- **Request accessibility consultation** for your projects
- **Include accessibility requirements** in project planning
- **Participate in accessibility testing** and user research
- **Share accessibility wins** and challenges with the practice

## Not Yet Working for CivicActions?

### Career Opportunities
CivicActions is always looking for talented people who share our mission of improving government digital services and creating more inclusive technology.

**Current opportunities**: Check [CivicActions Careers](https://civicactions.com/careers) for open positions

**Accessibility-related roles we hire for**:
- **Accessibility Engineers** - Technical implementation and testing
- **UX Designers** - Inclusive design and user research  
- **Content Strategists** - Plain language and accessible content
- **Developers** - Full-stack development with accessibility expertise
- **Project Managers** - Leading accessible digital transformation

## Contributing Guidelines

### For All Contributions
- **Focus on practical guidance** that helps people implement accessibility
- **Include real examples** and case studies when possible
- **Cite authoritative sources** like WCAG, Section 508, and accessibility experts
- **Write in plain language** that's accessible to diverse audiences

### Code Contributions
- **Follow accessibility best practices** in any code contributions
- **Test with assistive technology** when adding interactive features
- **Include accessibility considerations** in pull request descriptions
- **Document accessibility decisions** and rationale

### Content Contributions  
- **Verify links and resources** are current and accurate
- **Include context** about why resources are recommended
- **Consider diverse user needs** in examples and recommendations
- **Maintain consistent voice** and style with existing content

## Questions?

**For CivicActions team members**: Reach out through internal Slack channels or contact accessibility practice leads directly.

**For external contributors**: Open an issue in the relevant GitHub repository or use our [contact form](/about/contact/).

**For career opportunities**: Visit [CivicActions Careers](https://civicactions.com/careers) or email **hello@civicactions.com**.
