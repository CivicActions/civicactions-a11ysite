# Onboarding Staff to Accessibility

A comprehensive guide for organizations to effectively onboard new staff on accessibility principles and practices.

## Why Accessibility Onboarding Matters

Effective accessibility onboarding ensures all team members understand their role in creating inclusive digital experiences from day one. It builds organizational accessibility capacity and prevents costly remediation later.

## Onboarding Framework

### Week 1: Foundations
**Goal:** Understand why accessibility matters and basic concepts

**Activities:**
- Introduction to disability and accessibility concepts
- Review organizational accessibility policies and commitments
- Basic WCAG overview and success criteria
- Meet the accessibility team/champions

**Resources:**
- WebAIM Introduction to Web Accessibility
- Organizational accessibility statement and policies
- Team introductions and contact information

### Week 2: Role-Specific Training
**Goal:** Learn how accessibility applies to specific job functions

**For Developers:**
- Semantic HTML and ARIA basics
- Keyboard navigation requirements
- Screen reader testing introduction
- Automated testing tool setup

**For Designers:**
- Color contrast requirements
- Focus indicators and visual design
- Accessible typography and layout
- User research with people with disabilities

**For Content Creators:**
- Plain language principles
- Alternative text guidelines
- Document accessibility basics
- Accessible social media practices

**For Project Managers:**
- Accessibility requirements gathering
- Testing and QA considerations
- Timeline and resource planning
- Client communication strategies

### Month 1: Practical Application
**Goal:** Apply accessibility knowledge to actual work

**Activities:**
- Shadow experienced team members on accessibility tasks
- Complete accessibility review of existing project
- Participate in accessibility testing session
- Contribute to accessibility documentation

### Ongoing: Continuous Learning
**Goal:** Stay current with accessibility practices and standards

**Support Systems:**
- Monthly accessibility check-ins
- Access to training resources and conferences
- Connection with accessibility communities
- Regular skill assessment and development planning

## Assessment and Competency

### Knowledge Checkpoints
- Basic accessibility concepts (Week 1)
- Role-specific skills (Week 2)  
- Practical application (Month 1)
- Ongoing competency reviews (Quarterly)

### Skills Development
Track progress in key accessibility competencies and provide targeted support for skill gaps.

## Resources for Success

### Internal Resources
- Accessibility style guides and standards
- Testing tools and environment access
- Mentorship and buddy system connections
- Documentation and knowledge base access

### External Resources
- Professional development opportunities
- Community meetups and conferences
- Online training platforms and certifications
- Industry newsletters and updates

## Measuring Success

Effective onboarding programs show measurable improvements in:
- Accessibility issue prevention in new projects
- Staff confidence in accessibility tasks
- Time to competency for new hires
- Overall organizational accessibility maturity
